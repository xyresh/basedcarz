The MG TF




The MG TF, a controversial Car in the community.

It is a Mid engined RWD Roadster kind of like the Toyota MR-2 and many Supercars(clearly not as powerfull)

Powered by an underwhelming 1.7L Rover K series engine That Produces 150-ish Horsepower and about 120 pound feet of torque.

underwhelming Performance doesn't mean the car is Slow as it does 0-62 mph in about 7 seconds. nothing to bring home about but is not bad by any metric.

But as we have stated many times before understressed straight line speed does not make a car boring or "Not Based".The light chassis of this car alows it to be a joy to drive

coupled with the RR layout provides a driving experience unlike any other.

Unfortunately this car came in the era of the death of british industry and the manufacturing plants in Birmingham stopped operations in 2005.

the result was that manufacturing was brought over to china and that cost in build quality and reliability.

This is essentially one of the Last True British Roadsters as from that point and beyond everything was becoming german, chinese , indian and etc.



Also this is one of my personal Favourites and is especially close to heart for a close friend of mine.


So what makes this car BASED??


>Lightweight chassis
>True British Car (before 2005 of course)
>Good Handling
>Simplistic early 2000's equipment, the essentials only can be found
>Rear Drive Rear Mounted engine!!
>Cheap buying cost and relatively low running costs(uses standard Rover Parts which can still be found in europe)

So why not give this car a go??

may be tricky to maintain in the future due to the death of rover
